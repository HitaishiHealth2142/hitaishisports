About The Sport

Archery is one of the oldest arts still practiced today. The evolution of archery began at the start of mankind's history, and evidence of ancient archers has been found around the world.

Modern competitive archery involves shooting arrows at a target from a set distance. The two main types are target archery, where competitors shoot at stationary circular targets at varying distances, and field archery, which involves shooting at targets of varying distance, often in woodland and rough terrain.

In Olympic archery, competitors use recurve bows to shoot at targets 70 meters away. The target is 122 cm in diameter with a 10-ring measuring just 12.2 cm in diameter. Archers shoot 72 arrows in the qualification round, with the total score determining their rankings for the matchplay eliminations.
