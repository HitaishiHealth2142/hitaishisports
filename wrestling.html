    Wrestling Styles

    Freestyle Wrestling

    The Olympic style that allows holds below the waist and emphasizes throws and leg attacks. Points are awarded for takedowns, exposures, and throws.

    Greco-Roman Wrestling

    The other Olympic style that prohibits holds below the waist, focusing on upper body throws and clinch work. Known for spectacular suplexes.

    Folkstyle Wrestling

    The American collegiate style that emphasizes control and riding time. Features unique scoring for escapes, reversals, and near falls.

    Women's Wrestling

    Featured in the Olympics since 2004, women compete in freestyle wrestling with weight classes adapted for female athletes.
