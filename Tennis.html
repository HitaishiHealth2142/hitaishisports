EQUIPMENT GUIDE

Choosing the Right Racket

Finding a tennis racket that fits your game style is crucial. Factors like head size, grip thickness, frame stiffness, and string tension all impact your performance. Beginners should go for lighter rackets with larger heads, while advanced players often prefer smaller, control-oriented rackets.

Tennis Shoes & Court Surfaces

Tennis shoes are designed for specific court types — hard court, clay, or grass. Hard court shoes offer durability and cushioning, clay court shoes provide traction and support for sliding, while grass court shoes have nubs for grip. Always match your shoes to your playing surface for maximum performance and injury prevention.

Strings & Tension Guide

The type and tension of your strings greatly affect spin, control, and power. Polyester strings are popular among advanced players for durability and spin, while multifilament strings offer more comfort and power. Tension-wise, higher tension provides more control, whereas lower tension increases power.
