Karate Legends

Gichin Funakoshi

Founder of Shotokan karate, known as the father of modern karate.

Mas Oyama

Founder of Kyokushin karate, famed for his strength and discipline.

Rika Usami

World kata champion, renowned for her technical precision and artistry.

Rafael Aghayev

Multiple-time world kumite champion, considered one of the greatest competitors.

Signature Techniques

Kata

Formal patterns of movements that teach fundamental techniques, balance, and spirit.

Kumite

Sparring practice, focusing on timing, distance, and application of techniques in combat.

Iconic Karate Venues

Nippon Budokan

Tokyo's martial arts arena, host of Olympic karate and world championships.

Accor Arena

Paris' venue for international karate events and world championships.

Maracanãzinho

Rio de Janeiro's arena, host of international karate competitions.

Karate Through the Ages

From Okinawan roots to Olympic recognition, karate has inspired generations with its philosophy of respect, perseverance, and self-improvement.

1922

Karate Introduced to Japan

Gichin Funakoshi demonstrates karate in Tokyo, spreading the art throughout Japan.

1949

Japan Karate Association Founded

The JKA is established, standardizing karate practice and competitions.

1970

First World Karate Championships

The inaugural world championships are held in Tokyo, marking karate's global reach.

2021

Olympic Debut

Karate is included in the Olympic Games for the first time in Tokyo.
