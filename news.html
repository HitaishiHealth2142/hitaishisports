    2026 – Aronimink, PA

    Aronimink Golf Club in Pennsylvania will host with precision greens and classic fairways — a true test of tradition and skill. Known for its Donald Ross design, the course will demand accuracy and finesse as players navigate tight landing zones and undulating terrain under championship pressure. Its tree-lined layout and small greens make every shot critical. Historically, Aronimink has produced drama in the closing holes. Players will need discipline to attack pins without risking bogeys. Weather could also play a significant role. Fans can expect an intense, old-school challenge. The course favors players with strong iron play and a solid short game, especially around tricky bunkers guarding the greens. In recent years, Aronimink has become a favorite stop for top professionals, appreciated for its balance between challenge and fairness. The clubhouse offers stunning views of the Philadelphia skyline, enhancing the tournament’s atmosphere. Spectators will enjoy close access to key holes, ensuring an engaging live experience. Preparations for 2026 have focused on maintaining course integrity while improving fan amenities. This venue promises a memorable chapter in PGA history.

    02

    2027 – PGA Frisco, TX

    Frisco's Fields Ranch East brings a cutting-edge layout to the PGA calendar with its premier hosting debut. As the new home of PGA of America, the course will combine innovation and strategic challenges, giving rising stars and seasoned pros an exciting and unpredictable test. Designed by Gil Hanse, it blends modern aesthetics with historical roots. Wide fairways contrast sharply with heavily bunkered greens. Texas winds will likely influence club selection and distance control. The layout rewards creative shot-making. Fans will witness a true modern major. The venue also features advanced practice facilities, including state-of-the-art simulators and putting greens, enhancing player preparation. The local community is gearing up for a golf festival atmosphere, with fan zones, clinics, and interactive exhibits planned. Sustainability initiatives in course maintenance are a highlight, demonstrating PGA’s commitment to environmental stewardship. Media coverage will be extensive, with innovative broadcast technology capturing every shot. This tournament will showcase the future of golf.

    03

    2028 – Olympic Club, CA

    San Francisco’s iconic Olympic Club will test players with tight fairways and breezy Pacific conditions. The club, rich in championship history, challenges even the best with severe slopes and unpredictable winds that have turned leads into heartbreak in past major events. Its Lake Course has hosted legends and surprises alike. The venue punishes inaccuracy off the tee and on approach. Recovery shots are rarely easy. Cold coastal air reduces distance, further complicating club selection. Fans will enjoy breathtaking views and intense Sunday finishes. Course conditions tend to favor tactical play over power, with penal rough and tricky doglegs requiring strategic planning. The rugged coastal environment tests players’ mental toughness and shot creativity. The city’s vibrant culture adds to the excitement, with plenty for visitors to explore outside the ropes. Olympic Club remains one of the most revered venues on the major circuit.

    04

    2029 – Baltusrol, NJ

    The Lower Course at Baltusrol returns to the spotlight with its timeless championship setup. Known for its rich legacy and meticulously maintained fairways, Baltusrol blends beauty and brute challenge — demanding precision tee shots and nerves of steel on its lightning-fast greens. This A.W. Tillinghast masterpiece has produced thrilling finishes. Water hazards and bunkers come into play frequently. Success here requires elite short-game skill. A strong mental game is just as vital. Expect a leaderboard filled with experienced, disciplined competitors. The course’s layout encourages aggressive but calculated play, with several holes offering risk-reward options that could shift momentum quickly. The historic clubhouse and surrounding gardens offer a grand setting, making Baltusrol a favorite for players and fans alike. Spectators appreciate easy walking routes and excellent viewing vantage points, making the tournament a fan-friendly experience. Efforts to preserve the course’s heritage continue with modern agronomy techniques.

    05

    2030 – Congressional, MD

    Redesigned for modern majors, Congressional's rolling landscape and hazards promise drama in 2030. Set in the heart of Bethesda, the course features challenging elevation changes, water-protected greens, and a legacy of unforgettable moments in PGA history. With recent renovations by Rees Jones, it now boasts a bolder design. The risk-reward par-5s offer scoring chances but punish errors. Precision off the tee is crucial. Green complexes demand proper angles of approach. Expect the final stretch to deliver pure championship drama. Congressional is famous for its intimidating back nine, where pressure mounts and champions are forged. The course’s wide fairways may mislead; hazards and thick rough catch errant shots easily. Spectators will be treated to a classic test of golf intelligence and endurance. Local support and volunteer enthusiasm add to the tournament’s energy. Preparation and planning for 2030 are already underway to ensure an unforgettable event.
