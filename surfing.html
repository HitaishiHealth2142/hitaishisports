Surfing Legends

Duke Kahanamoku

Father of modern surfing, Olympic swimmer, and Hawaiian icon.

Kelly Slater

11-time world champion and greatest competitive surfer of all time.

Laird Hamilton

Big wave surfing pioneer and innovator of tow-in surfing.

Stephanie Gilmore

7-time world champion and one of the most dominant female surfers.

Surfing Styles

Shortboarding

The most common and dynamic form of surfing, performed on boards 5-7 feet long, allowing for quick turns and aerial maneuvers.

Longboarding

Traditional style using boards 8-12 feet long, emphasizing smooth, graceful maneuvers and nose riding.

Iconic Surfing Locations

Pipeline, Hawaii

The most famous and dangerous wave in the world, known for its perfect barrels.

Jeffreys Bay, South Africa

One of the world's best right-hand point breaks with long, perfect waves.

Teahupo'o, Tahiti

Feared for its heavy, thick barrels breaking over shallow coral reef.

Surfing Through the Ages

From its Polynesian roots to its modern professional circuits, surfing has evolved into a global phenomenon and Olympic sport.

1778

First European Record

Captain James Cook observes surfing in Hawaii, the first European documentation.

1950s

Modern Surfing Begins

Lightweight fiberglass boards revolutionize surfing, making it more accessible.

1976

First Pro Tour

The International Professional Surfers (IPS) forms the first world championship tour.

2021

Olympic Debut

Surfing makes its Olympic debut at the Tokyo 2020 Games.
